## Overview
Google Sites is a no-code website builder that integrates with Google Workspace tools such as Drive, Docs, Sheets, Forms, Calendar, Maps, and YouTube. It works well for amateur radio clubs that need a simple, collaborative, and easy-to-maintain website. <a href="https://support.google.com/sites/answer/6372878?hl=en&ref_topic=7184580" target="_blank">support.google.com</a>

This guide introduces the key concepts club operators and web contributors need to build and maintain a Google Sites website. This guide was build using Microsoft Co-Pilot and Google GEMINI AI products.

This information is provided as a guide to link to training resources for readers. The reader is responsible to understand material and any actions taken to apply this information.

---

## 1. What Google Sites Is (and Isn.t)

### Key Concepts

- No-code, drag-and-drop website builder.
- Good for static content, club information, event pages, training resources, and embedded Google Workspace content. <a href="https://support.google.com/sites/answer/6372878?hl=en&ref_topic=7184580" target="_blank">support.google.com</a>
- Integrates with Docs, Sheets, Slides, Forms, Drive, YouTube, Calendar, and Maps. <a href="https://support.google.com/sites/answer/90569" target="_blank">support.google.com</a>
- Supports collaborative editing and controlled sharing. <a href="https://support.google.com/sites/answer/6372878?hl=en&ref_topic=7184580" target="_blank">support.google.com</a>
- Does **not** provide full custom HTML/CSS page editing like a traditional web development platform.
- Publishing is quick and updates can be republished as changes are made. <a href="https://support.google.com/sites/answer/6372878?hl=en&ref_topic=7184580" target="_blank">support.google.com</a>

## 8. Best Practices for Ham Radio Clubs

### Recommended Workflow

- Use Google Drive folders to organize club documents.
- Use Google Forms for:
  - Event RSVPs
  - Volunteer sign-ups
  - Training registration
- Use Google Sheets for:
  - Rosters
  - Net logs
  - Event logs
- Embed YouTube playlists for training videos.
- Use Google Calendar for recurring meetings and events.
- Keep navigation simple and predictable.
- Assign at least two backup editors.
- Use consistent page names and layout patterns so future volunteers can maintain the site easily.

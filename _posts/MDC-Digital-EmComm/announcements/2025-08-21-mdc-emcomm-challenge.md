## Purpose

In the September Challenge, you will get experience creating an initial assessment of damages in your local community, to assist disaster recovery operations by local authorities. The internet and cell towers will be simulated down, as can be expected after major disasters, such as earthquakes, floods, tornados, wild fires, or volcanos. You will gain experience using peer-to-peer (P2P) communications to send messages to a local emergency operations coordinator, relying solely on your RF capability. Our Challenges afford an opportunity to maintain radio operator proficiency, which can only be done with trial and error and repeated practice. Gaining proficiency with various messaging software, mastery of your equipment, and experience with the vagaries of radio wave propagation are key elements of the Challenge.

For our VarAC users, this exercise should be done by ?parking? the message at a Target Station. Parking a message that is addressed to a fellow amateur radio operator is a special case that adds some flexibility beyond a simple relay of the message. If the intended recipient station?s VarAC beacon is detected by a Target Station holding a parked message for him, an alert notification is automatically generated by VarAC notifying the addressee that there is a message available for pickup. This feature can be particularly valuable to a Target Station that may be handling a large amount of traffic in an unattended mode, by allowing traffic to move without manual intervention (see PARKING VMAIL FOR RELAY / PICKUP below).

## Planning

- If you are a mobile or portable station operator, you really should have GPS capability to take advantage of the built-in locator features in Winlink and VarAC. If you do not have GPS already, I suggest Googling for a USB GPS Puck and procuring one from your favorite vendor and plugging it into your computer. 

- To support multiple applications accessing your GPS's comm port, such as your time sync and messaging applications, Enterlogic's VSPE (Virtual Serial Port Emulator), is a solution. It provides a special virtual splitter function that can support up to 10 applications. As an alternative to VSPE,  com0com  shareware is available. Info on how to set up com0com can be found at: https://mcacs.net/training-resources/gps-hockey-puck/ 

- Winlink: Set up your GPS / Position Report settings. See how to at https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=MAg-iP0A1D0. 
Also, go to Settings > Preferences > Message sending options, and check the box for: Include your location in message headers. This will automatically provide your location in the headers of all of your Winlink messages. 

- VarAC: Go to Settings > Rig control and VARA Config > GPS Tab > Set up the comm port that your GPS is using. Put the tag <GPSLOC> in your messages to describe your location.

- No GPS? You can get an accurate location for your messages from Google Maps, https://www.google.com/maps/. Enter your address in the search bar > left click on pin for details > right-click on the detailed map's pin > the coordinates are at the top of the pop-up list > left click and they will be copied to the clipboard > paste these coordinates into your message.

## Task 1

, Winlink: Use the Winlink Initial Damage Assessment form found under General Forms for your initial observations.  Use your local Jurisdiction (County or City). Incident: as indicated above. Mark as Exercise. Event: Hurricane. Survey Area: geographic coordinates. Survey Team: your group ID. Use the WINDSHIELD ASSESSMENT SURVEY checklist (attached) to estimate the extent of observed simulated damages and total count in each category. The $ Loss column should be left blank. In the Comments block, provide a description of the observed geographical boundaries, i.e streets, rivers, community name, etc., and describe the observed damages in general terms (example below). Submit  form. Mark the message that is created as a P2P message and address it to a Target Station. Post it to your outbox. Open a P2P Session with the Target Station. The message will automatically transfer to the Target Station who will relay.

Example Comments: Bowie, MD vicinity of Whitehall Dr. from Old Chapel Road to MD 197. Initial observations.  Many trees down, many side streets are impassible. Many homes sustained Minor wind damage and at least 3 homes are uninhabitable with Major damage caused by falling trees. A few homes in low lying areas appear to have sustained minor damage from flooding.


## Task 2

, VarAC: Before making your connection to a Target Station, prepare your message for parking: Use EmComm mode >SEND VMAIL > Load Template >  ICS-213 General Message > address To Callsign: N3XL > Subject: Initial Damage Assessment - EXERCISE  > in MESSAGE block use Incident Name, Subject, and Message Addressee, as indicated above > for item 7. use the WINDSHIELD ASSESSMENT SURVEY checklist (attached) and provide the following:  a. general description of the observed geographical boundaries, i.e streets, rivers, community name, etc., b. describe the observed simulated damages, c. provide geographic coordinates > approve your message and SEND  > the message will post to your Outbox > use Advance mode to make your connection to theTarget Station and park the message (see procedures below). 

Be aware that EmComm mode must be selected to access the ICS-templates. In exercises your transmissions should be done in Advanced mode, reserving EmComm mode for real emergencies.

PARKING VMAIL AT A TARGET STATION FOR RELAY / PICKUP:
Before making a VarAC connection with a Target Station, it is recommended to prepare the message for relay/pickup. To open message templates, use EmComm mode and select the SEND VMAIL button. Address the message to the ultimate destination's callsign. Enter desired text. Once prepared, select the SEND button to post in Outbox. Connect to a Target Station. Once connected, go to your Outbox, right click on the message, and select "Relay now through connected station".

## Flmsg Tips

(Recommended NBEMS Config attached):
a. Save your ICS-213 in Flmsg using ?File > Save?. 
b. You may view sent and received messages, File > Open C:\Users\userx\NBEMS.files\ICS\messages. 
c. To send again, use ?File > Open? and double-click on the message > AutoSend. 
d. Once your message is open, you will find File > View > Plain Text to be a handy way to copy the message text. 

ESTABLISHING FLDIGI CONNECTIONS (P = Participant. TS = Target Station):
P - Tune radio to TS dial frequency. Use QSY button to adjust frequency to the 1500 sweet spot.
TS - QST QST de WX3F WX3F, QST QST de WX3F WX3F K (call periodically, QST=calling all stations).
P - Precisely align cursor to TS signal, to obtain good copy. The display center frequency will normally be at, or near, 1500. 
P - Once good copy of the Target Station is established, respond WX3F WX3F de KB3XYZ KB3XYZ, I have traffic K
TS - RR KB3XYZ good copy, pls send your traffic K.
P ? Send message using AutoSend.
TS ? Brief acknowledgement, or request to resend, etc.

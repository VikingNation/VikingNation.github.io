## Planning

Imagine a disaster such as a hurricane, tornado, terror attack, or other natural disaster knocking out all internet and cellular communications in your community. What role will you play during in this event? What equiment will you be using?

Ensure that your VARA, VarAC, Fldigi, and Winlink software programs are up-to-date.
VARA - https://rosmodem.wordpress.com
VarAC - https://www.varac-hamradio.com
Winlink - https://www.winlink.org/WinlinkExpress
Fldigi/Flmsg - https://sourceforge.net/projects/fldigi/files/

Winlink Packet P2P and Vara FM P2P users should consider the use of digipeating to extend their range beyond VHF line-of-sight. A VARA license is required to digipeat using Vara FM.

VarAC FM Stations should ensure that the following QSO tab check-boxes are left blank:
- Exchange Call ID and SNR after QSY from CF
- Consider entire frequency list as CF
- Auto QSY
- Allow Band skip QSY
- Send QSY Freq Offset

VarAC FM users should are encouraged to edit of the VarAC FM Frequency Drop-down list to reflect only the digipeaters/target stations that you are able to use. For example:
145.010.000|W3AAC-11
145.090.000|WM3M-10
145.750.000|N3XL-10
145.770.000|KF3AK-10
441.000.000|KF3AK-11


## Execution

INCIDENT: MDC DECEMBER CHALLENGE
SUBJECT: CALLSIGN, EMERGENCY READINESS RESPONSE
DATES: 00:00, 4 DEC 2025 to 23:59, 7 DEC 2025
TIMES: EST (ET)


## Task 1

. Using Winlink, carefully perform the following:
a. Ensure that in your Winlink 'Preferences' settings that the 'Message sending options' check box for 'Include your location in message headers' is checked.
b. Create an ICS-213 message form addressed To: Emergency Coordinator, KB3SPH, Subject; Your CALLSIGN, EMERGENCY READINESS RESPONSE.  
c. Place the chosen Emergency Response Role (only one), associated equipment, supported modes, and your deployment preference selected for this exercise (see below).
d. Send your message via P2P direct, P2P via digipeater, or P2P relay via Target Station to KB3SPH.
e. Radio-Only messages may be sent via any Radio-Only capable RMS station.


## Task 2

. Use VarAC VMail and carefully perform the following:
a. Access an ICS-213 message form in EmComm mode. Address To: Emergency Coordinator, KB3SPH, Subject; Your CALLSIGN, EMERGENCY READINESS RESPONSE.  
b. Place your chosen Emergency Response Role, associated equipment, supported modes, and your deployment preference (see below).
c. Switch to ADVANCED mode and send your message direct, via digipeater, or relay via a Target Station to KB3SPH. To relay via a Target Station, parking the message at the relay station is the preferred method (see below). 

Be aware that EmComm mode must be selected to access the ICS-templates. In exercises your transmissions should be done in Advanced mode, reserving EmComm mode for real emergencies.

PARKING VMAIL AT A TARGET STATION FOR RELAY / PICKUP:
Before making a VarAC connection with a Target Station, it is recommended to prepare the message. To open message templates, use EmComm mode and select the SEND VMAIL button. Address the message to the ultimate destination's callsign. Enter desired text. Once prepared, select the SEND button to post in Outbox. To park the message, connect to a Target Station, go to your Outbox, right click on the message and select "Relay now through connected station".


## Task 3

. Use Fldigi/Flmsg and carefully perform the following:
a. Create an ICS-213 message form addressed to Emergency Coordinator, KB3SPH, Subject; Your CALLSIGN, EMERGENCY RESPONSE.  
b. Place your chosen Emergency Response Role, associated equipment, supported modes, and your deployment preference (see below).
c. Send directly, or via an available Target Station. Request relay to KB3SPH. 

 EMERGENCY RESPONSE ROLE AND ASSOCIATED EQUIPMENT (Select only one): 
- Hand-held Radio Operator: Radio(s) amateur bands, max power, modes: analog voice, DMR.
 
- Mobile Station: Radio(s), antenna(s), amateur bands, max power. Modes: analog voice, DMR, APRS, CW, Winlink, VarAC, Fldigi. 

- Portable Station: Radio(s), antenna(s), amateur bands, max power. Modes: analog voice, DMR, CW, Winlink, VarAC, Fldigi. 

- Fixed Station: Radio(s), antenna(s), amateur bands, max power. Modes: analog voice, DMR, CW, Winlink, VarAC, Fldigi.

- Support role at a Fixed Station, Hospital Site, EOC. or elsewhere.

 DEPLOYMENT PREFERENCE :
- ARES Hospital Emergency Radio Operator (HERO)
- ARES Field Deployment
- ARES Home Station 
- MARS
- SHARES
- Civil Air Patrol
- CERT 
- RRI/DTN
- Other first responders

* ESTABLISHING FLDIGI CONNECTIONS (P = Participant. TS = Target Station)
P - Tune radio to TS dial frequency. Use QSY button to adjust frequency to the 1500 sweet spot.
TS - QST QST de WX3F WX3F, QST QST de WX3F WX3F K (call periodically, QST=calling all stations).
P - Precisely align cursor to TS signal, to obtain good copy. The display center frequency will normally be at, or near, 1500. 
P - Once good copy of the Target Station is established, respond WX3F WX3F de KB3XYZ KB3XYZ, I HAVE IMAGE FOR RELAY TO N3XL K.
TS - RR KB3XYZ good copy, pls send your picture K.
P ? Send image. Terminate the transmission when image xfer is completed.
TS ? Brief acknowledgement, or request to resend, etc.

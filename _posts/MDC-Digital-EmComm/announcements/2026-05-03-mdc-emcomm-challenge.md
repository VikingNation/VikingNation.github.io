## Scope
MDC Digital EMCOMM Challenges provide a training opportunity for Winlink, VarAC, and/or Fldigi radio operators to sharpen their skills for service in the Amateur Radio Emergency Service (ARES), or in other public service organizations. Challenges simulate internet down scenarios, such as natural disasters or terrorist attacks, and primarily use peer-to-peer (P2P) messaging protocols that require relays. Winlink VARA FM, VARA HF, and HF Radio-only are supported. VarAC HF and VarAC FM provide feature rich EMCOMM platforms.  Fldigi HF messaging is incorporated for interoperability with ARES groups that use the ARRL Narrow Band Emergency Messaging System (NBEMS).

## Purpose

In the MAY Challenge you will simulate participation in an ARES Mutual Assistance Team (ARESMAT) event and provide Health and Welfare (H&W) status messages from areas experiencing extensive power and communications outages caused by a hurricane or tornadoe. Amateur radio operators often serve an important role by relaying H&W messages from disaster areas. (People outside a disaster area are strongly discouraged from sending welfare inquiries into a disaster area. Such inquiries can overwhelm limited communications channels, including Amateur Radio networks, and should be directed to the Red Cross.) You will gain familiarity with Winlink and VarAC message templates and the standard NTS Radiogram format available in Fldigi to send H&W messages.


## Task 1

, Winlink: You are simulating an ARESMAT operator deployed to a hurricane or tornado disaster zone. Use either the RRI Radiogram ICS-213 ,or the RRI Welfare Radiogram, template and create a H&W status report message from a person in the disaster zone. Ensure that all available addressee information is provided in the message to facilitate message delivery. Incorporate originator information, incident name, subject: and at least 3 RRI Gold message texts (see attached) in the message.  Use a Target Station?s callsign in the Liaison Station field. Send your message.


## Task 2

, VarAC: You are simulating an ARESMAT operator deployed to a hurricane or tornado disaster zone. In EmComm mode use VMail to access the ICS-213 message form and compose a H&W status report message from a person in the disaster zone. Ensure that all available addressee information is provided in the message to facilitate message delivery. Incorporate originator information, incident name, subject: and at least 3 RRI Gold message texts (see attached) in the message. In this case the addressee holds an amateur radio callsign, so you should address the VMail to that callsign, N3XL. Park your message at a Target Station. (see PARK VMAIL FOR RELAY/PICKUP below). When the addressee has no callsign, as would usually be the case, you would send the Vmail to a Target Station who will be responsible for manually relaying the message.

PARKING VMAIL FOR RELAY/ PICKUP:
Before making a VarAC connection with a Target Station, prepare the message. Use EmComm mode to access the ICS-213 template. Address the form to the formal message addressee. Enter desired text. Once prepared, address the Vmail using the intended recipient?s callsign if they have one. Otherwise, you just send the 213 to the relay station. Post to your Outbox. Connect to a Target Station in VarAC Advanced or Simple mode. Once connected, go to your Outbox, right click on the message, and select "Relay now through connected station."  Note: EmComm mode should only be used for transmissions in a real emergency, but it needs to be used to open the message form.


## Task 3

, Fldigi/Flmsg: You are simulating an ARESMAT operator deployed to a hurricane or tornado disaster zone. Follow these instructions carefully, to create an NTS compatible Radiogram. Use the ARRL Radiogram form found in FLMSG and create a H&W status report message from a person in the disaster zone. Include all of the Addressee and Originator information provided above and Op Note: EXERCISE MESSAGE. Place the Incident Name provided above and at least 3 ARL numbered messages seleted using the ARL MSG button. Use the Insert "X" between fields checkbox. Do not select a Liaison Station, use the Target Station callsign to be used for relay in the Liaison Station field. Connect to the Target Station and when the connection is established, verify that Flmsg is in correct mode setting and click on AutoSend found in the top tabs. Wait on frequency for a message acknowledgment from the Target Station.


## Flmsg Tips

(Recommended NBEMS Config attached).
-. Save your Radiogram in FLMSG using ?File > Save?. 
-. You may view sent and received messages, File > Folders > NBEMS files\ICS\messages.
- To send again, use ?File > Open? and double-click on the message > AutoSend. 
- Once your message is open, you will find File > View > Plain Text to be a handy way to copy the message text. 

* ESTABLISHING FLDIGI CONNECTIONS (P = Participant. TS = Target Station)
P - Tune radio to TS dial frequency. Use QSY button to adjust frequency to the 1500 sweet spot.
TS - QST QST de WX3F WX3F, K (call periodically, QST=calling all stations).
P - Precisely align cursor to TS signal, to obtain good copy. The display Center Frequency will normally be at, or near, 1500. 
P - Once good copy of the Target Station is established, respond WX3F WX3F de KB3XYZ KB3XYZ, I HAVE TRAFFIC FOR RELAY TO N3XL K.
TS - RR KB3XYZ good copy, pls send your traffic K.
P ? Send traffic. Terminate the transmission when xfer is completed.
TS ? Brief acknowledgement, or request to resend, etc.
ext.

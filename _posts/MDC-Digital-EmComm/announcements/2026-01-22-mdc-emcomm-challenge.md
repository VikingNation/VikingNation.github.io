## Scope

MDC Digital EMCOMM Challenges provide a training opportunity for Winlink, VarAC, and/or Fldigi radio operators to sharpen their skills for service in the Amateur Radio Emergency Service (ARES), or in other public service organizations. Challenges simulate internet down scenarios, such as natural disasters or terrorist attacks, and primarily use peer-to-peer (P2P) messaging protocols. Fldigi messaging, is incorporated for interoperability with ARES groups that use the ARRL Narrow Band Emergency Messaging System (NBEMS). If you do not want to use Winlink you are welcome to participate using VarAC and/or Fldigi, but must send your commercial email address to bill.n3xl@verizon.net, to be added to the MDC Winlink distribution list. The commercial email option is not open for Winlink users.

## Task 3

Fldigi HF (mode MFSK32) Image Xfer: 
a. Review the image transfer training at https://drive.google.com/file/d/11WT6GyQkOqavS0-ejTaqqcBO4KkEoJOG/view?usp=sharing . 
b. Establish a connection with your Target Station, see ESTABLISHING FLDIGI CONNECTIONS below.
c. In blue transmit area of the screen, right click and select ?Send Image?? Press the "Load" button in the dialog window and select one of your resized images. The file selection dialog has a preview capability so you will see what the image looks like. 
d. Send your picture via a Target Station for relay, or directly to N3XL. After sending secure the Tx/Rx function using the Rx button.
e. If the transmission fails using X1, be prepared to resize the image using the X2 button, or go to a gray scaled image in the dialog window. X2 will shrink the image size at the receiving end.
f. If relaying via a Target Station, type RELAY TO N3XL in the SEND window, before or after sending the image.

* ESTABLISHING FLDIGI CONNECTIONS (P = Participant. TS = Target Station)
P - Tune radio to TS dial frequency. Use QSY button to adjust frequency to the 1500 sweet spot.
TS - QST QST de WX3F WX3F, K (call periodically, QST=calling all stations).
P - Precisely align cursor to TS signal, to obtain good copy. The display center frequency will normally be at, or near, 1500. 
P - Once good copy of the Target Station is established, respond WX3F WX3F de KB3XYZ KB3XYZ, I HAVE TRAFFIC FOR RELAY TO N3XL K.
TS - RR KB3XYZ good copy, pls send your traffic K.
P ? Send traffic. Terminate the transmission when xfer is completed.
TS ? Brief acknowledgement, or request to resend, etc.


## Planning

File size limitations to achieve reasonable RF transmission times will vary, depending on the quality of your connection, propagation conditions, and equipment limitations. The nominal file size limit for Winlink and VarAC files is 100 Kbytes, which is too large for RF transmissions. We are suggesting that you resize your images below 30 Kbytes for Vara FM and 15 Kbytes for HF, and make further size adjustments if the transmission fails or takes too long.
You are encouraged to send images to as many Target Stations as possible. The HF and VHF/UHF Target Station lists will be provided to everyone on the MDC Winlink distribution list on 3 FEB 2026. Carefully observe the modes, frequencies, dates, and times of operation provided.

Winlink Vara FM P2P and VarAC FM users should consider the use of digipeating to extend their range beyond VHF line-of-sight. A VARA license is required to digipeat using Vara FM. To unlock all of the latest features and optimal performance of the software, a license is recommended.
Ensure that your VARA, VarAC, Fldigi, and Winlink software programs are up to date.
VARA - https://rosmodem.wordpress.com
VarAC - https://www.varac-hamradio.com
Winlink - https://www.winlink.org/WinlinkExpress
Fldigi - https://sourceforge.net/projects/fldigi/files

VarAC FM Stations should ensure that the following QSO tab check-boxes are left blank:
- Exchange Call ID and SNR after QSY from CF
- Consider entire frequency list as CF
- Auto QSY
- Allow Band skip QSY
- Send QSY Freq Offset

VarAC FM users are encouraged to edit of the VarAC FM Frequency Drop-down list to reflect only workable digipeater frequencies. For example:

## Purpose

The August Challenge will be a message relay exercise. You will gain familiarity with ICS-213 General Message forms found in Winlink, VarAC, and FLMSG. You will also get experience using the attached damage assessment checklist, which was extracted from Montgomery County, MD, Fire and Rescue Service WINDSHIELD ASSESSMENT SURVEY. You will create an ICS-213 message providing descriptions of the affected location, and the level and extent of simulated damages. Target Stations will perform relays or hold your messages for pickup.

## Execution

INCIDENT NAME: MDC EMCOMM CHALLENGE
SUBJECT: TORNADO DAMAGE ASSESSMENT - EXERCISE 
TIMES: EDT (ET)


## Task 1

Winlink messaging. Use an ICS-213 general message template.  Use the checkbox provided on the form to mark your message as Exercise traffic. Use Incident Name and Subject as indicated above. Address the form to AEC-OPS@arrl.net. Place the affected location and simulated damages assessment, in block 7. Send the message to a Target Station. The Target Station will perform the necessary relay. In the case of an EMCOMM Post Office, the message will be held for pick-up by N3XL.


## Task 2

VarAC messaging. Utilize EMCOMM mode. Before making your connection to a Target Station, prepare your message. Procedure: SEND VMAIL > LOAD TEMPLATE > choose ICS-213 General Message template > use Incident Name and Subject as indicated above. > place the affected location and simulated damages assessment in block 7 > address the form to AEC-OPS@arrl.net > address the VMAIL to N3XL > park the VMail at the VarAC Target Station for pickup or relay. Please study how to park a VMAIL, before sending ? you may not find the process intuitive (see 'PARK VMAIL FOR RELAY' below).


## Task 3

Fldigi messaging. Use an ICS-213 form found in the FLMSG application. Use Incident Name and Subject as indicated above. Place the affected location and simulated damages assessment in the Message block, address the form to AEC-OPS@arrl.net > make your connection to a Target Station. Verify your modem type in FLMSG and Fldigi match up, and send your message using FLMSG ?Autosend?. Your message will be acknowledged by the Target Station and forwarded, as necessary. 

- PARK VMAIL FOR RELAY:
1. Before making a VarAC connection with a Target Station, it is recommended to prepare (SEND VMAIL button) the message for relay, including the ultimate recipient in the TO: box. Once prepared, post the VMail to your Outbox. Once you are connected to your Target Station, go to your Outbox, right click, and select 'Relay now through connected station'. 
2. For short messages that require no preparation ahead of time (or if you have a copy ready to go), you can connect to a Target Station, click on the SEND VMAIL button, and address to N3XL. The system will ask if you want to 'Relay now through connected station'.


## Challenge Credit

N3XL will provide message acknowledgements to participants for Tornado Damage Assessments that have been successfully delivered or picked up. Participants are encouraged to complete all three tasks in the August Challenge, but do what you can. You are encouraged to connect and send to as many Target Stations as you can. Participation will be documented in the MDC Section EMCOMM Challenge Report and in the ARRL MDC Section Newsletter.  * HIT A HOMERUN: Everyone completing all 3 tasks will be recognized for hitting a homerun.

 In this exercise your message acknowledgement will contain a scorecard, as follows:
?	Your message was/ was not marked an EXERCISE message.
?	Your message adequately/ inadequately described the LOCATION where simulated damages occurred.
?	You message effectively/ ineffectively described both the LEVEL AND EXTENT, of simulated damages.

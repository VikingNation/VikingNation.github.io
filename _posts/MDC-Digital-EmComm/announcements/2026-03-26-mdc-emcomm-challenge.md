## Scope

MDC Digital EMCOMM Challenges provide a training opportunity for Winlink, VarAC, and/or Fldigi radio operators to sharpen their skills for service in the Amateur Radio Emergency Service (ARES), or in other public service organizations. Challenges simulate internet down scenarios, such as natural disasters or terrorist attacks, and primarily use peer-to-peer (P2P) messaging protocols that require relays. Winlink VARA FM, VARA HF, and HF Radio-only are supported. VarAC HF and VarAC FM provide feature rich EMCOMM platforms.  Fldigi HF messaging is incorporated for interoperability with ARES groups that use the ARRL Narrow Band Emergency Messaging System (NBEMS).


## Purpose

In the APRIL Challenge you will gain familiarity with the National Weather Service local forecast services. You will note any specific Hazardous Weather Warnings and the 48-hour forecast provided. You will practice editing the forecast to reduce the word count to speed the message transmission times. (see example below).

## Example Of A Local Wx Forecast

Use the forecast provided by your local forecast office found at the National Weather Service, https://www.weather.gov/. Click on the Text Only Forecast hyperlink located under ?Additional Forecasts and Information? at the bottom of the local forecast page. Copy and paste any Hazardous Weather Advisories and forecast for the next 48 hours into a text editor. Use abbreviations and eliminate redundant or vague information. Remember, this is a forecast not a guarantee. Try to clearly convey information with the fewest words possible, to achieve the shortest transmission time.

## Task 1

Winlink: Use an ICS-213 general message template and provide your edited local weather advisory (if any) and 48-hour weather forecast. Place the forecast in block 7 of the ICS-213, address the form to the above message addressee and send as a Winlink VARA HF or VARA FM peer-to-peer (P2P) message to a Target Station for relay and/or send via Winlink HF Radio-only RMS directly to N3XL.


## Task 2

VarAC: Use an ICS-213 general message template and compose a VMail containing your edited local weather advisory (if any) and 48-hour weather forecast. Address the ICS-213 form to the message addressee above. Address the Vmail to N3XL and park it at a VarAC Target Station for pickup/relay. Please study how to park a Vmail, before sending ? you may not find the process intuitive (see below).

PARKING VMAIL FOR RELAY/ PICKUP:
Before making a VarAC connection with a Target Station, prepare the message. Use EmComm mode to access the ICS-213 template. Address the form to the formal message addressee. Enter desired text. Once prepared, address the Vmail using the intended recipient?s callsign if they have one. Otherwise, you just send the 213 to the relay station. Post to your Outbox. Connect to a Target Station in VarAC Advanced or Simple mode. Once connected, go to your Outbox, right click on the message, and select "Relay now through connected station."  Note: EmComm mode should only be used for transmissions in a real emergency, but it needs to be used to open the message form.


## Task 3

Fldigi messaging. Use the ICS-213 general message form found in Flmsg and place your edited local weather advisory (if any) and 48-hour weather forecast in the message block. Make your connection to a Target Station. Verify that your modem type in FLMSG and Fldigi match up (MFSK32). Send your message using Flmsg ?Autosend? at the top of the form. Your message will be acknowledged by the Target Station and relayed to the addressee.

d. Messages can be found using Windows Explorer.  Open C:\Users\userx\NBEMS.files\ICS\Messages).

* ESTABLISHING FLDIGI CONNECTIONS (P = Participant. TS = Target Station)
P - Tune radio to TS dial frequency. Use QSY button to adjust frequency to the 1500 sweet spot.
TS - QST QST de WX3F WX3F, K (call periodically, QST=calling all stations).
P - Precisely align cursor to TS signal, to obtain good copy. The display Center Frequency will normally be at, or near, 1500. 
P - Once good copy of the Target Station is established, respond WX3F WX3F de KB3XYZ KB3XYZ, I HAVE TRAFFIC FOR RELAY TO N3XL K.
TS - RR KB3XYZ good copy, pls send your traffic K.
P ? Send traffic. Terminate the transmission when xfer is completed.
TS ? Brief acknowledgement, or request to resend, etc.

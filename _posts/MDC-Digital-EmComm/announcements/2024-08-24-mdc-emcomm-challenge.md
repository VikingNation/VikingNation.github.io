## Purpose

In the September Challenge you will simulate participation in an ARES Mutual Assistance Team (ARESMAT) event and provide Health and Welfare (H&W) status messages from a simulated tornado disaster zone. In this Challenge participants will gain familiarity with Radiogram and/or ICS-213 Forms and practice Winlink, VarAC, and Fldigi Peer-to-Peer (P2P) messaging.  Since different ARESMAT teams use differing messaging software, it is important to gain familiarity with as many of them as you can. It is only with trial and error and repeated practice that operator proficiency can be maintained. 

The emphasis on P2P messaging in Challenges is because when the internet goes down in our region, and Ham communications services are called upon, this will be the primary method that will be available. HF Radio-only (RO) services and Winlink EMCOMM Post Office services may also be available and should be exercised from time to time. 

This exercise introduces Radio Relay International (RRI) Gold ?safe and well? texts for use in Winlink?s RRI ICS213 Radiogram template, VarAC?s ICS-213, and the FLMSG Radiogram. For additional background, RRI reference materials are available at  https://radiorelay.org/about/ . 

SCENARIO:
A simulated EF4 Tornado has devasted communities from LaPlata to Waldorf, MD, traversing near Route 301. First responders are on scene, the internet is down in the area. ARESMAT assistance has been requested. Winlink, VarAC, and Fldigi Target Stations will be providing H&W message relays of message traffic from the disaster zone.

## Task 1

Winlink messaging: You are simulating an ARESMAT operator deployed to a Tornado disaster zone. Under RADIOGRAM & RRI form templates, select the RRI ICS213 Radiogram template and create a H&W status report message from a person in the disaster zone (see attached Radiogram Instructions). Use the Precedence, Place of Origin, Local time, To: and From: address info, and select message text from Standard RRI GOLD texts (see attached). Click on the button to preview for accuracy before submitting. Send your Radiogram as a Winlink P2P message to a Target Station for relay, or if available, drop it off at the WX3F EMCOMM Post Office. In this Challenge, you will need to get your message to a Target Station. The RRI Liaison stations listed as tactical addresses in the drop-down at the bottom of the form are not used in this Challenge. In a real world event, select the Liaison Region that is associated with the addressee's location.


## Task 2

VarAC messaging: You are simulating an ARESMAT operator deployed to a Tornado disaster zone.  In EMCOMM mode, compose a Vmail ICS-213 form containing a H&W status report message from a person in the disaster zone. On the ICS 213 form, use the To: and From: address and Subject: info provided below, Use message text selected from Standard RRI GOLD texts (see attached). Approve the message with your name and callsign. Send Vmail addressed to N3XL and park it at a VarAC Target Station for relay (see PARK VMAIL FOR RELAY below).


## Task 3

Fldigi messaging: You are simulating an ARESMAT operator deployed to a tornado disaster zone. Use the RADIOGRAM Form found in FLMSG and create a H&W status report message from a person in the disaster zone. Use Precedence and Place of Origin provided below. The Hx box should be left blank for this exercise. Comp check-box should be left blank for this short Radiogram. The ARL MSG button should not be used to create this message. Leave the Standard Format check-box blank. Use Joe Schmo in the SIG: box. Put EXERCISE H&W MESSAGE in both OP NOTE boxes. Place message text selected from Standard RRI GOLD texts(see attached) AND the originator's Contact Info in the TXT box. Click on red ck button when TXT is completed. When ready, click on AutoSend in the top tabs and send your message to a Fldigi Target Station for relay. Wait on frequency for a short message acknowledgment from the Target Station.

- PARK VMAIL FOR RELAY:

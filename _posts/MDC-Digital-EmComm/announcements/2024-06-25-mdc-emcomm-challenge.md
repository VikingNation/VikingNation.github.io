## Purpose

The July Challenge will be a message relay exercise. In addition to Winlink, VarAC, and Fldigi messaging, this Challenge is designed for participants to gain familiarity with National Weather Service (NWS) local forecast office products. Besides the plain language weather forecast information needed in this exercise, it is suggested that you examine the current conditions (humidity, barometer, dewpoint, etc.) FAQs, and other information found under various hyperlinks available on the NWS web site.


## Planning

Develop a 24-48 hour forecast for your zip code from detailed forecast information found in the Forecast > Local tab of the National Weather Service web site https://www.weather.gov/erh/.

You are encouraged to send messages to as many Target Stations as possible. The HF and VHF Target Station lists will be provided to everyone on the MDC Winlink distribution list on 2 JULY 2024. Carefully observe the modes, frequencies, dates, and times of operation provided.

Winlink Packet P2P and Vara FM P2P users should consider the use of digipeating to extend their range beyond VHF line-of-sight. A VARA license is required to digipeat using Vara FM.

Ensure that your VARA, VarAC, Fldigi, and Winlink software programs are up-to-date.
VARA - https://rosmodem.wordpress.com
VarAC - https://www.varac-hamradio.com
Winlink - https://www.winlink.org/WinlinkExpress
Fldigi - https://sourceforge.net/projects/fldigi/files/

INCIDENT: MDC DIGITAL EMCOMM CHALLENGE

TIMES: EDT (ET)


## Task 1

Winlink messaging. Use an ICS-213 general message template. Place your local 24-48-hour weather forecast in block 7, address the form to N3XL@arrl.net, and send it to a Target Station for relay. In the case of the WX3F EMCOMM Post Office, the message will be held for pick-up by N3XL.


## Task 2

VarAC messaging. Before making your connection to a Target Station, prepare your local 24-48-hour weather forecast. Procedure: SEND VMAIL > LOAD TEMPLATE > choose ICS-213 General Message template > place your local 24-48-hour weather forecast in block 7 > address the form to N3XL@arrl.net > address the VMAIL to N3XL > park the VMail at the VarAC Target Station for relay. Please study how to park a VMAIL, before sending ? you may not find the process intuitive (see 'PARK VMAIL FOR RELAY' below).


## Task 3

Fldigi messaging. Use an ICS-213 form found in the FLMSG application. Place your local 24-48- hour weather forecast in the Message block, address the form to N3XL@arrl.net, make your connection to a Target Station. Verify your modem type in Flmsg and send your message using ?Autosend?. Your message will be acknowledged by the Target Station.


## Example Of A Local Wx Forecast

Use the NWS Weather Prediction Center, local forecast office at https://www.weather.gov/erh/  for your zip code. It is suggested that you develop your forecast using the ?Text Only Forecast? version, using the link found at the bottom of the page, and edit for brevity. Use abbreviations and eliminate redundant information.

- PARK VMAIL FOR RELAY:
1. Before making a VarAC connection with a Target Station, it is recommended to prepare (SEND VMAIL button) the message for relay, including the ultimate recipient in the TO: box. Once prepared, post the VMail to your Outbox. Once you are connected to your Target Station, go to your Outbox, right click, and select 'Relay now through connected station'. 
2. For short messages that require no preparation ahead of time (or if you have a copy ready to go), you can connect to a Target Station, click on the SEND VMAIL button, and address to N3XL. The system will ask if you want to 'Relay now through connected station'.
